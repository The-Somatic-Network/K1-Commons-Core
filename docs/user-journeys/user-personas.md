# User Personas

This document captures the five primary user personas for the K1 Commons Core platform.

---

## 1. The Explorer

**Who they are:** A new user who has never interacted with the platform. They want to understand what the platform offers before committing time or money.

**Goal:** Discover the value proposition and available services.

**Journey:**
1. Lands on the platform homepage.
2. Browses categories and featured co-ops.
3. Reads about the solidarity model and cooperative structure.
4. Creates an account after seeing enough value.

**Pain Points:**
- Information overload — too many categories with no clear guidance.
- Unclear value proposition — doesn't understand the co-op model.
- Trust barriers — hesitant to create an account without proof of legitimacy.

**Success Criteria:** The user creates an account and follows or bookmarks at least one service listing.

---

## 2. The Buyer (Service Seeker)

**Who they are:** A user actively looking for services — can be a first-time buyer or a returning customer.

**Goal:** Find a qualified provider, negotiate terms, and purchase a service.

**Journey:**
1. Searches or filters by skill, location, or category.
2. Reviews provider profiles, ratings, and past contracts.
3. Initiates contact and negotiates scope and price.
4. Funds an escrow contract and tracks progress.

**Pain Points:**
- Inconsistent quality across providers.
- Difficulty comparing providers without standardized reviews.
- Escrow friction — users worry about fund safety and dispute resolution.

**Success Criteria:** The buyer successfully completes at least one transaction with a clear contract and escrow release.

---

## 3. The Provider (Service Seller)

**Who they are:** A new user who wants to offer their skills on the platform. This could be a developer, designer, writer, or organizer.

**Goal:** Create a service listing, attract clients, and earn income.

**Journey:**
1. Creates a profile with skills and portfolio samples.
2. Publishes service listings with pricing and scope.
3. Responds to buyer inquiries and accepts contracts.
4. Delivers work, receives escrow payout (95% to them, 5% to solidarity fund).

**Pain Points:**
- Standing out in a crowded marketplace.
- Unclear expectations from buyers leading to scope creep.
- Payment delays or disputes.

**Success Criteria:** The provider publishes at least one service listing and receives their first contract.

---

## 4. The Returning Buyer (Status Checker)

**Who they are:** A long-term user checking on the status of ongoing services they previously purchased.

**Goal:** Monitor progress, review deliverables, and manage active contracts.

**Journey:**
1. Logs in and navigates to their dashboard.
2. Reviews active contracts and delivery timelines.
3. Communicates with the provider on progress updates.
4. Releases escrow upon satisfactory completion.

**Pain Points:**
- Poor visibility into project progress — no real-time status updates.
- Missed deadlines or silent providers.
- Dispute resolution is unclear or slow.

**Success Criteria:** The buyer reviews all active contracts and confirms or requests changes to each.

---

## 5. The Returning Provider (Status Updater)

**Who they are:** A long-term provider updating clients on the status of services they are currently delivering.

**Goal:** Communicate progress, manage expectations, and get paid.

**Journey:**
1. Logs in and navigates to active contracts.
2. Updates each contract with progress milestones and deliverables.
3. Receives client feedback and addresses revisions.
4. Marks contracts complete to trigger escrow release.

**Pain Points:**
- Clients who don't respond to updates or payment triggers.
- Scope changes mid-project without updated contracts.
- Lack of a structured way to log progress and communicate deliverables.

**Success Criteria:** The provider updates all active contracts and successfully triggers at least one escrow release.
