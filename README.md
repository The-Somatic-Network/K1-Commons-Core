# K1-Commons-Core

## The Systemic Problem
Under modern capitalism, digital freelance platforms (like Fiverr and Upwork) extract between 10% to 40% of a worker’s labor value just to enrich distant Wall Street shareholders. This structure isolates independent workers, forces them into rigid corporate hierarchies, and drains wealth away from local communities.

## Our Open-Source Solution
We are coding a decentralized, borderless Freelance Marketplace and Co-op Service Registry. This platform will allow international developers, designers, writers, and organizers within our network to sell their digital labor directly to ethical buyers, labor unions, and other cooperatives globally.

## Non-Custodial Alignment (The Solidarity Fund)
The remaining 5% platform fee accumulates automatically in our server’s wallet. In accordance with council communist principles, this fund operates with a non-custodial rule: We will use the pooled capital exclusively to launch and finance new, independent worker councils and local cooperatives (such as community land trusts, local tool-shares, or clean energy grids). Once these new cells are stable, we leave them completely alone. We extract zero equity and hold zero authority over them, maintaining a flat federation of free equals.
